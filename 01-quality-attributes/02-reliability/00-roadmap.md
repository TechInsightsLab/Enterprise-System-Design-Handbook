# Chapter 2: Reliability

This chapter builds directly on High Availability.

While High Availability focuses on **keeping the system accessible**, Reliability focuses on **producing the correct result consistently over time**.

Many engineers incorrectly use these terms interchangeably. One of the goals of this chapter is to clearly distinguish:

* High Availability
* Reliability
* Durability
* Fault Tolerance
* Resilience
* Disaster Recovery

We'll answer questions such as:

* Why does Reliability exist?
* Why do businesses invest in Reliability?
* Can a system be Highly Available but Unreliable?
* Can a system be Reliable but unavailable?
* How do architects design Reliable systems?
* How is Reliability measured?
* What architectural patterns improve Reliability?
* What are common Reliability failures?
* How do large companies like Amazon, Google, Stripe, Netflix, and Uber approach Reliability?
